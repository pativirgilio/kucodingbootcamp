Obesity in the USA

Smoking and obesity are major public health challenges and the prevalence of both is high globally. Smoking increases the risk of cancer, respiratory and cardiovascular diseases, and is the leading preventable cause of death in developed countries./ Obesity is the fifth leading cause of death, globally, and is closely linked to cases of diabetes. There is a general perception that poverty may influence obesity rates and smoking habits.


The data above interactively shows the results of research on this topic. It draws attention to the States of Arkansas and West Virginia as the most obese States in the USA with also high rates of smokers. However, it seems that poverty has not a strong correlation with obesity, as we see high obesity rates accross low and high poverty levels.
